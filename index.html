About Me

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the School of Computing at Clemson University. My advisor is Prof. Hongxin Hu. I received my M.S. in Computer Science from Clemson University and M.E. in Software Enginnering from University of Science and Technology of China. I am currently a research assistant in network and system security, and a teaching assistant for graudate level computing and networking security courses at Clemson University.

Research

My research goal is to develop a platform that can provide better protection to networks and systems utilizing emerging computing and networking technologies. In particular, I explore appraoches to developing network security funtions, such as intrusion detection systems (IDSes) and firewalls, taking the advatages of network function virtualization (NFV) and software-defined networking (SDN). I envision those software defined programmable network security functions can not only protect both traditional networks/systems and virtualized environments, but also secure the emerging edge computing environments for IoT. Furthermore, I also investigate effective ways to exploit artificial neural networks (ANNs) to enchance the capabilities of existing network security functions.
